The Many Uses of an SBOM: A Cloud Practitioner's Perspective

The views and opinions expressed in this article are those of the author and do not reflect the views of any organization or employer.

Software Bill Of Materials (SBOMs) are essential for modern software environments. While implementation challenges exist, the trajectory is clear: SBOMs are becoming a non-negotiable component of software security and regulatory compliance. Organizations that embrace them proactively will gain significant advantages in risk management, incident response, and customer trust. Here's why they matter to those of us building and securing cloud environments:

Visibility into Supply Chain, Distributed Components and Infrastructure as Code

In containerized and serverless environments, tracking what's actually running across your infrastructure can be difficult and expensive without proper automation and tooling. SBOMs give you instant visibility into every component across your Kubernetes clusters, serverless functions, and microservices. They help answer the question: "What's actually in production right now?"

Similarly, when your infrastructure is defined as code, SBOMs help you identify vulnerable dependencies in your Terraform modules, CloudFormation templates, or Pulumi stacks. By integrating SBOM generation into your IaC pipelines, you can catch security issues before they reach your cloud environment.

Those container images you're pulling from Docker Hub or ECR? An SBOM reveals exactly what's inside, including base images, installed packages, and applications. This allows organizations to analyze supply chain risk across every dependency.

This visibility is invaluable when investigating security events to quickly determine if suspicious behavior is related to vulnerable components and prioritize responses.

The integration of SBOMs into vulnerability management can also dramatically reduce Mean Time To Remediate (MTTR) and alert fatigue. Teams can focus on actual exploitable vulnerabilities rather than wasting time determining whether tickets are findings versus false positives, making remediation efforts more efficient and targeted. When the next Log4Shell happens (and it will), having current SBOMs means you can immediately identify every affected resource.

Cloud Cost Optimization

Beyond security, SBOMs help identify redundant or outdated components consuming cloud resources. By eliminating unnecessary dependencies, you can reduce container sizes, decrease memory footprints, and ultimately lower your monthly cloud bill.

SBOM generation fits into existing CI/CD approaches. With free, open source tools like Grype, you can automatically generate SBOMs during container builds and gate deployments based on security findings without adding any friction or undue cost.

Security Metrics That Matter

SBOMs enable metrics that resonate with executive leadership, such as overall supply chain risk, Mean Time To Remediate (MTTR) and the number of unique vulnerabilities across an entire inventory. These metrics help justify security investments and demonstrate the effectiveness of a security program far beyond traditional compliance checkboxes.

SBOMs bridge the visibility gap between cloud infrastructure and security controls, enabling organizations to protect modern environments at the speed and scale of cloud development. Cloud security and engineering leaders need practical security solutions that work at scale without compromising the speed and flexibility that makes cloud computing powerful. SBOMs provide exactly that.
